“Oka has a certain kind of aura about it, because it was the place of the blacksmiths that created implements which made agriculture possible.” Chinua Achebe.






The current spelling of Oka is “Awka”, to make it clear what I am writing about. Thereafter, I have reverted to the old spelling “Oka”, The change in the spelling of the name from “Oka” to “Awka” was made by the British Colonial Office in 1908. According to the researches of Dr. Nwibe Onejeme, Barrister-at-Law, of Umuokpu Village, the change of spelling was made by the Colonial Secretary, Mr. F.S. James, in 1908, through Government Notice No. 729, published in the Southern Nigerian Gazette of 1908, No. 42, Volume 3 at page 8511.
